    Nov 4th¶

    Tuesday

    Bloomberg¶

    from Bloomberg

    Five Things Need to Know

    • The global stock rally hit a speed bump as earnings from AI bellwether Palantir failed to impress and Wall Street chiefs warned of a correction due to rich valuations. Bitcoin headed for the lowest level since June.
    • Palantir shares are slumping almost 8% in premarket trading. The company raised its revenue forecast and outpaced analyst estimates for third-quarter sales, but that didn’t reassure investors worried about its lofty valuation after a record run-up.
    • Starbucks agreed to sell a majority stake in its China business to private equity firm Boyu Capital at a $4 billion enterprise value in a bid to improve the coffee chain’s flagging fortunes in the country.
    • First Brands Group sued founder Patrick James for allegedly misappropriating hundreds of millions of dollars from the US automotive supplier. The firm collapsed into bankruptcy in September in a case that rattled credit markets.
    • BP’s profit exceeded expectations with operational improvements and higher oil and gas production outweighing lower prices, as the company’s turnaround plan builds momentum.

    US Market Preview¶

    from S&P Capital IQ

    Stock futures stumbled Tuesday, with contracts tied to Nasdaq-100 leading declines and a distinct note of caution creeping into markets. Wall Street's fear gauge, the VIX index of stock volatility, jumped.

    Palantir's stock dropped premarket , despite the data-analytics company reporting another quarter of record revenue.

    The recent rally has made stocks vulnerable to a pullback, Morgan Stanley Chief Executive Ted Pick and other finance bosses told a conference in Hong Kong on Tuesday, according to media reports.

    Among the risks: trade tensions, bubble worries and trouble in credit markets.

    Investors have also been unsettled by indications that the Federal Reserve might not cut interest rates again next month.

    On Monday, Governor Lisa Cook described the central bank's December meeting as "live." Mary Daly and Austan Goolsbee also said they had open minds as they weighed a slowing labor market against above-target inflation.

    Vice Chair Michelle Bowman is due to speak Tuesday.

    Meanwhile, if the government shutdown continues through tonight, it will surpass the 35-day record set during Trump's first term.

    Stocks to Watch

    Denny's agreed to be taken private in a $322 million deal. Shares rose 49% premarket.

    Jeld-Wen said it would cut its North American and corporate workforce by about 11% and consider a sale of its European business. Shares fell 14%.

    Sarepta Therapeutics swung to a loss and reported two of its therapies didn't meet statistical significance for a recent trial's primary endpoint. The stock slid 38%.

    Sanmina guided for profit and revenue above analyst estimates. Shares rose 13%.

    Tesla was down almost 2.5% after a major investor rejected Musk's $1 trillion pay deal.

    Market Wrap¶

    from S&P Capital IQ

    Tuesday’s equity session turned into a broad pull-back as investors took profits from 2025’s AI-driven surge and fretted over deepening government-shutdown disruptions. Heavy selling in megacap technology names steered the mood: Nvidia skidded more than 3 percent and Palantir sank over 7 percent after upbeat but heavily scrutinized results triggered valuation worries, helping to drag the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite to a loss of 2.04 percent for the day NBC News. Caution spread beyond Silicon Valley as warnings from Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley about a looming correction kept buyers on the sidelines, and liquidity jitters tied to the record-long shutdown further undercut sentiment. By the closing bell the Dow Jones Industrial Average shed 0.53 percent, the S&P 500 slipped 1.17 percent, and the Nasdaq posted its worst single-day slide since July.

    Corporate headlines offered little relief. IBM became the latest blue chip to trim payrolls, announcing plans to cut a “low single-digit” slice of its 270,000-strong workforce despite reporting stronger-than-expected software revenue earlier this quarter CNBC. Elsewhere, Amazon won fresh applause after OpenAI inked a seven-year, $38 billion agreement to run ChatGPT workloads on AWS, bolstering the retailer’s position in the cloud arms race even as executives across Wall Street warned of an AI capex bubble. In M&A chatter, Papa John’s tumbled 18 percent after word surfaced that Apollo Global had quietly withdrawn its $64-per-share bid, underscoring how financing costs are cooling private-equity appetites Seeking Alpha.

    Commodities offered no safe harbor. Brent crude settled down 0.7 percent at $64 a barrel and WTI slid 0.8 percent to $60 as a firmer dollar and weak factory data amplified fears that the OPEC+ decision to pause output hikes signals looming oversupply Reuters. Gold, usually a refuge during equity turbulence, slipped more than 1 percent to near $3,930 an ounce, pressured by that same dollar strength and fading hopes for a December Fed rate cut Reuters. Risk aversion spilled into digital assets as Bitcoin broke below $104,000 for the first time since June, a move a veteran trader likened to “IPO-style distribution” as early holders rotated into AI and Chinese equities Benzinga.

    Agricultural markets delivered their own drama: Arabica coffee reversed early gains to close lower after the Brazilian real weakened, spurring export hedging from growers, while robusta futures faltered on expectations of record Vietnamese supply NASDAQ.

    Headlines¶

    from Yahoo Finance

    Burry Disclose Bets Against Nvidia, Palantir after Bubble Warning

    Key Points from the News

    • Burry’s Positioning: Through his hedge fund, Scion Asset Management, Burry revealed short bets against Nvidia (NVDA) and Palantir (PLTR).
    • Bubble Warning: He has repeatedly cautioned that AI enthusiasm is inflating valuations beyond reasonable expectations, similar to past tech bubbles.
    • Market Context:
    • Nvidia has been the poster child of the AI boom, with its stock soaring on GPU demand.
    • Palantir has rallied on AI software adoption and government contracts.
    • Investor Reaction: Burry’s moves highlight growing concerns that AI equities may be overextended, even as broader markets remain bullish.

    Takeaway¶

    Metric (as of late 2025) Nvidia (NVDA) Palantir (PLTR)
    Market Cap ~$4.9 trillion ~$471 billion
    Trailing P/E 57.7 658.1
    Forward P/E 35.1 267.7
    Price/Sales (P/S) 29.9 127.7
    PEG Ratio ~0.98 (growth-adjusted, reasonable) ~7.2 (very stretched)
    Growth Outlook Strong near-term EPS growth from AI GPU demand Revenue growth, but profitability thin; valuation assumes massive future adoption

    • Nvidia:

      • Valuation is high but partially justified by explosive earnings growth from AI chip demand.

      • Forward P/E of ~35 suggests investors expect continued strong growth, but at least earnings are catching up.

      • PEG ratio near 1.0 indicates valuation is not wildly out of line with growth.

    • Palantir:

      • Valuation is extreme: forward P/E above 250 and P/S above 120.

      • PEG ratio >7 shows growth expectations are far outpacing realistic earnings delivery.

      • Market is pricing Palantir as a winner-takes-all AI software platform, which is highly speculative.
