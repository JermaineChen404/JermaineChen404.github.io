# Short-Term Financial Planning
Firms require short-term financing for

- Seasonality
- Negative cash flow shocks
- Positive cash flow shocks

## The Matching Principle
- Permanent Working Capital (minimum level of working capital)
	- long-term sources of funds
- Temporary Working Capital
	- short-term sources of funds

## Financing Policy Choices
### Aggressive Financing Policy
Use more short-term debt

>[!warning] Funding Risk
>The risk of incurring financial distress costs if the firm cannot refinance timely or at a reasonable rate

>[!check] Benefit
>- short term debt is less sensitive to the firm's credit quality
>	- lower funding cost
>- lower rates
>	- yield curve usually have positive slope

### Conservative Financing Policy
Use more long-term sources of funds

>[!warning] Cost
>- Excess cash earns below-market interest rate
>- interest income on the cash reduces interest tax shield
>- agency problem

## EAR in General
$$EAR=\left(\frac{\text{Final Payment}}{\text{Initial Proceeds}}\right)^{1/T}-1$$
