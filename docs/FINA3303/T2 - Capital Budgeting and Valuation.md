## WACC method
>Discount the unlevered free cash flows using the weighted-average cost of capital
### Net Working Capital
$$\begin{align}
\text{Net Working Capital}&=\text{Current Assets}-\text{Current Liability} \\
&=\text{Cash}+\text{Inventory}+\text{Account Receivables}-\text{Account Payables}
\end{align}
$$

- Short-term interest-bearing liabilities are not included 
	- e.g. note, debt
	- account payables do not bear interest
	- analyze the project without the influence of financing decisions
### Unlevered Free Cash Flow
$$\text{FCF}=\text{EBIT}(1-T_{c})+\text{Depre.}-\Delta \text{NWC}-\text{Cap Ex}$$

- Income Statement: EBIT, Depreciation, Capital Expenditure
- Balance Sheet: Net Working Capital
### Value of the Firm
![[Pasted image 20250922001258.png]]

$$\sum \frac{\text{Unlevered FCFF}_{n}}{(1+\text{WACC})^n}=\text{Enterprise Value}=E+D-\text{Cash}$$

- Cash flow are computed assuming no leverage
- leverage effects are only reflected in the WACC
	- [[T1 - Capital Structure#Proposition II - WACC|WACC incorporates interest tax shield]]
- Interest expense is assumed to be zero

Alternatively,

$$\sum \frac{\text{CF to Equity}}{(1+R_{e})^{n}}=E=\text{Enterprise Value}-D+\text{Cash}$$
### Assumption
1. maintain constant debt-equity ratio and WACC is constant
	- different debt-equity ratio, different WACC
2. the investment has comparable risk to the rest of the firm
	- discount cash flow from different projects with same WACC
