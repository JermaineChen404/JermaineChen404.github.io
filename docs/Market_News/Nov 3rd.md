### Five Things Need to Know
- The [rally in US stocks](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-02/stocks-set-for-mixed-open-opec-to-pause-hikes-markets-wrap?srnd=homepage-europe&sref=rvrmfDby) is set to continue, with investors gearing up for Palantir earnings today and a slate of private economic reports for fresh direction on the outlook for interest rates. [Oil slipped](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-02/latest-oil-market-news-and-analysis-for-nov-3?srnd=undefined&sref=rvrmfDby) and the dollar was steady.
- China’s manufacturing activity expanded less than forecast in October, a private survey showed, adding to [worrying signs about the economy’s momentum](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-03/china-factory-activity-growth-decelerates-private-survey-shows?sref=rvrmfDby) as the year winds down.
- Nigeria’s dollar bonds slumped after [Donald Trump threatened military action](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-03/nigerian-assets-tumble-after-trump-threatens-military-action?srnd=homepage-europe&sref=rvrmfDby) if the country doesn’t halt killings of Christians by Islamist militants. Nigeria’s Bola Tinubu rejected Trump’s characterization, saying he was disregarding the government’s “consistent and sincere efforts’’ to safeguard religious freedom.
- Alphabet is returning to Europe’s debt market for the second time this year, [raising at least €3 billion](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-03/alphabet-to-sell-at-least-3-billion-bonds-to-fund-ai-expansion?srnd=undefined&sref=rvrmfDby), as the Google parent raises funds to support record capital spending on AI and cloud infrastructure.
- The [US government shutdown](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-01/shutdown-pain-spreads-at-one-month-touching-tens-of-millions?srnd=undefined&sref=rvrmfDby) became painfully real for tens of millions Americans as it hit the one-month mark over the weekend with food aid disrupted, cuts to child care kicking in, and health insurance premiums spiking.
