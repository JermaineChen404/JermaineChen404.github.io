>[!Note] Five Things Need to Know
>- A selloff in technology stocks dragged global markets lower for a second day as investors [pull back from the AI boom](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-05/global-chip-selloff-erases-500-billion-in-value-as-fears-mount). US futures recovered from their worst levels overnight and were down only slightly. [Bond yields fell](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-05/flight-to-treasuries-shows-fears-stocks-slump-has-room-to-run "Link") and Bitcoin steadied.
>- The US government shutdown dragged into [a record 36th day](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-05/longest-shutdown-tests-air-travel-federal-workers-and-patience) amid a Congressional stalemate over health-care and spending priorities. The [economic pain](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2025-11-05/record-government-shutdown-costs-us-economy-about-15-billion-each-week) is set to deepen as maneuvers to pay some government employees will run out this month.
>- Novo Nordisk trimmed its forecast for a fourth time this year on lagging sales of its blockbuster drugs [Wegovy and Ozempic](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-05/novo-cuts-outlook-once-more-on-lagging-ozempic-wegovy-sales?sref=rvrmfDby), underscoring the urgency facing its new chief executive amid competition from Eli Lilly.
>- Zohran Mamdani was elected mayor of New York, putting [an avowed democratic socialist](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-05/mamdani-wins-race-to-become-next-mayor-of-nyc-ap-says "Link") in charge of the city that serves as the capital of global financial markets. Democrats also won races for governor in [New Jersey and Virginia](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-05/2025-election-results-show-democratic-strategy-for-beating-trump).
>- Siemens Healthineers’ shares slumped 7% in Frankfurt after the medical-device maker warned the impact from Donald Trump’s tariffs [will double next year to €400 million](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-05/siemens-healthineers-sees-tariff-impact-doubling-in-fiscal-2026). It joins several companies this week warning of the impact from the levies.
