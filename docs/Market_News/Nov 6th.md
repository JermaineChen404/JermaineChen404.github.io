## Bloomberg 
> from [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/account/newsletters/markets-daily)

>[!Note] Five Things Need to Know
> - US companies announced [the most job cuts for any October in more than two decades](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-06/ai-revolution-prompts-most-october-us-layoffs-in-over-20-years?sref=rvrmfDby) as AI reshapes industries and cost-cutting accelerates, according to data from Challenger, Gray & Christmas. [Treasuries advanced](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-06/treasuries-rally-after-weak-us-jobs-data-spur-rate-cut-bets) on the news and traders boosted wagers on Fed interest-rate cuts.
>- Buy-the-dip [optimism faded from US stocks](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-05/stock-market-today-dow-s-p-live-updates), with stellar earnings doing little to lift sentiment as lofty tech valuations kept investors cautious. Futures for the S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 were little changed and European stocks slipped.
>- Donald Trump’s use of broad powers to impose his signature tariffs faced a barrage of [skeptical questions at the Supreme Court](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-06/supreme-court-s-trump-tariff-skepticism-means-uncertainty-reigns?sref=rvrmfDby), a signal the justices may be ready to intervene. Businesses are set for months of uncertainty whatever happens.
>- The US will [cut flight capacity by 10% in 40 high-volume markets](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-05/us-cutting-capacity-at-multiple-airports-as-shutdown-persists?sref=rvrmfDby) to alleviate pressure on air traffic controllers, who are working without pay during the longest government shutdown in history. International routes will be spared.
>- SoftBank explored [a potential takeover of US chipmaker Marvell](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-06/softbank-is-said-to-have-explored-potential-takeover-of-marvell) this year, people familiar with the matter said. SoftBank made overtures but the two sides couldn’t agree on terms, the people said. Marvell is up 8.7% in premarket trading.
